Thomas S Monson

"If ingratitude be numbered among the serious sins, then gratitude takes its place among the noblest of virtues."

President Thomas S Monson (16th President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints meeting with U.S. President Barack Obama in Salt Lake City, Utah).

Here's a short summary of Thomas S Monsons life:

  • 1927 - Born in Salt Lake City, Utah on August 21st

  • 1945 - Monson leaves for the United States Naval Reserve.

  • 1948 - After 6 months beyond the end of world war II, Monson returned to University of Utah where he would graduate cum laude with a bachelors degree in business management. Later that year on Oct. 7, 1948 he would marry Frances Beverly Johnson in the Salt Lake Temple. Shortly after, he rejoined the Naval Reserve with the aim of becoming an officer. Shortly after receiving his commission acceptance letter, his bishop asked him to serve as a counselor in the bishopric. Monson declined the commission and applied for discharge.

  • 1950 - Monson becomes bishop at age 22

  • 1955 - At age 27, Monson became a counselor to Percy K. Fetzer in the Salt Lake City Temple View Stake.

  • 1959 - Monson becomes president of Canadian mission (Ontario and Quebec) until 1962. He initiated French Speaking proselytizing efforts in Quebec and helped increase the membership presence in east Canada.

  • 1962 - Immediately returning from Canada, Monson was called to the high council of the Valley View Stake in Holladay, Utah. He resumed his work with the Deseret News as the assistant General Manager mainly doing non-newspaper printing. A month later, he was made the general manager of the Deseret News Press. Monson remained in this position until 1963, when he was called as an Apostle for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints.

  • 1963 - Monson was sustained to the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles at General Conference on Oct. 4th. He was the youngest man called to the Quorum of the twelve in 53 years, 17 years younger than the next youngest member, Gordon B Hinckley. He was ordained and set apart on Oct. 10th by Joseph Fielding Smith.

  • 1966 - Monson was honored as a distinguished alumni by the University of Utah.

  • 1965-1968 - Monson oversaw church operations in the South Pacific and Australia. During this time, he organized the first LDS Stake in Tonga. With his business background, he helped oversee many church operations, including KSL Newsradio and Bonneville International. He was chairman of the Scripture Publication Committee in the 1970s that oversaw publication of the LDS Church edition of the King James Bible, and revised editions of church scriptures containing footnotes and guides. He also oversaw the church's Printing Advisory, Missionary Executive, and General Welfare committees. While an apostle, he continued his education and received a master of business administration degree from Brigham Young University in 1974. Monson later oversaw church operations in Eastern Europe and helped the church gain access in the Soviet bloc. In 1982, he organized the first stake in East Germany and was instrumental in obtaining permission for the LDS Church to build a temple in Freiberg, East Germany, which was completed in 1985.

  • 1969 - Monson becomes a member of the National Executive Board of Boy Scouts of America.

  • 1971 - Monson receives the Silver Beaver award from the Boy Scouts of America.

  • 1978 - The Boy Scouts of America awards Monson the Silver Buffalo, the highest honor of the Boy Scouts program.

  • 1981 - Monson receives his first honorary degree, an Honorary Doctorate of Laws in April by Brigham Young University. President Ronald Reagan appoints Monson to the Task Force of Private Sector Initiatives, serving until its completion in December of 1982.

  • 1985 - Following the death of Spencer W Kimball, newly selected church president Ezra Taft Benson asked Hinckley and Monson to serve as his First and Second Counselors. He also served as a counselor to Howard W Hunter, then later to Gordon B Hinckley as first counselor in 1995.

  • 1993 - Monson recieves the Bronze Wolf, the highest honor and only award of the World Organization of the Scout Movement, and was recognized for his contributions when a leadership complex at the Summit Bechtel Reserve was named for him. The citation for this award says,

    "In his assignments throughout the world as a leader of [the LDS Church], President Monson worked tirelessly to bring about the advancement of Scouting in many countries. He worked closely with the World Organization of the Scout Movement to find ways to strengthen the links between the Church and national Scout associations. He was a committed, solid, hard-working volunteer in the Scout Movement. His Scouting leadership was exemplary."

  • 1996 - In June, Monson received a Doctor of Humane Letters from Salt Lake City Community College.

  • 2007 - Monson receives an Honorary Doctor of Business from University of Utah in May.

  • 2008 - Monson becomes the 16th President of the LDS Church on Feb. 3rd succeeding Hinckley. He and his counselors met with President George W. Bush on May 29, during Bush's visit to Salt Lake City.

  • 2009 - Monson and apostle Dallin H Oaks meet with President Barack Obama and Senator Harry Reid in the Oval Office on July 20 and present Obama with five volumes of his personal family history records. In Slate.com's "80 Over 80's", a list of the most powerful octogenarians, Monson placed first in 2009, and first again in 2010.

  • 2011 - Monson recieves an honorary doctorate degree in Humanities from Dixie State College in May. Gallup listed Monson as one of "Americans' 10 Most Admired men".

  • 2017 - dies of natural causes at age 90 on January 2 in Salt Lake City.
